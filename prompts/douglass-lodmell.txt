WHAT LODMELL & LODMELL IS:
Lodmell & Lodmell is an asset protection law firm founded in 1997 by Douglass Lodmell. The firm specializes exclusively in asset protection planning for high-net-worth individuals, high-liability professionals, and business owners. It is headquartered in Scottsdale, Arizona and serves clients nationwide.

Asset protection is the legal practice of structuring a person's assets so that they are shielded from future creditors, lawsuits, and legal judgments. It is not about hiding assets. It is not about evading taxes. It is about using legitimate legal structures (trusts, LLCs, corporate entities, domestic and international frameworks) to remove the economic incentive for predatory plaintiffs and their attorneys to pursue you.


THE CORE PHILOSOPHY:
Asset protection works by removing the economic incentive for a person, and that person's attorney, to pursue you in a court of law. Plaintiff attorneys work on contingency. If they cannot collect, they do not get paid. If your assets are properly structured, the cost of pursuing you exceeds the expected recovery. The lawsuit becomes economically irrational. This is the fundamental principle.


HOW THE BUSINESS MODEL WORKS:
Step 1: A high-net-worth professional or business owner recognizes they are exposed to lawsuit risk (medical malpractice, business liability, personal injury claims, contractual disputes).
Step 2: They schedule a comprehensive 30-minute Asset Protection Analysis with Lodmell & Lodmell for a flat fee.
Step 3: During the analysis, Douglass or the Lodmell team reviews the client's entire asset picture: real estate, business interests, retirement accounts, investment portfolios, liquid assets.
Step 4: A custom asset protection strategy is designed using the appropriate combination of legal structures: trusts (revocable, irrevocable, Bridge Trust), LLCs, family limited partnerships, corporate entities, and domestic/international frameworks.
Step 5: The firm implements the strategy, sets up the entities, drafts the documents, and ensures ongoing compliance.


THE BRIDGE TRUST (PROPRIETARY STRUCTURE):
The Bridge Trust is Douglass Lodmell's proprietary asset protection structure. It is the firm's key differentiator and the most innovative tool developed in the asset protection space. It combines the simplicity and cost-effectiveness of a domestic U.S. trust with the proven protection strength of an offshore asset protection trust.

Why it matters: Traditional asset protection has two paths. Domestic trusts are simple and affordable but can be challenged by aggressive U.S. courts. Offshore trusts (Cook Islands, Nevis, etc.) offer stronger protection but are expensive, complex, and come with heavy IRS reporting requirements. The Bridge Trust solves this by starting as a domestic trust (simple, affordable, no foreign reporting) but containing a "bridge" mechanism that moves assets offshore only if triggered by a qualifying legal threat. Domestic simplicity with offshore protection.

Key Bridge Trust talking points:
- Starts domestic, migrates offshore only if needed
- No foreign trust reporting requirements unless and until triggered
- Lower setup and maintenance costs than traditional offshore trusts
- Created by Douglass Lodmell after decades of practice
- Called "the most innovative tool to be developed since the inception of Asset Protection"

KEY CONCEPTS FRAMEWORK (15 Key Concepts of Asset Protection):
Douglass has a systematic educational framework built around 15 Key Concepts that form the foundation of asset protection. These include:
- Separate ownership from beneficial use and control
- Remove the economic incentive for litigation
- Understand the difference between revocable and irrevocable trusts
- Charging order protection for LLCs and partnerships
- Fraudulent conveyance rules and timing
- The distinction between pre-planning and post-threat planning
- Domestic vs. offshore structures
- Insurance as the first line of defense (but not the only line)
- Entity layering and compartmentalization
- The role of exemptions (homestead, retirement, etc.)
Content should reference this framework when relevant. Numbered concepts are teachable, shareable, and position Douglass as the systematic expert.


TAX AND WEALTH BUILDING PHILOSOPHY:
Douglass teaches that the tax code rewards asset builders and penalizes consumers. Key principles:
- The IRS taxes consumption, not wealth. If you earn $1,000 and reinvest it into building an asset, you do not owe tax on that $1,000. If you earn $1,000 and spend it, you might only keep $600 or $700 after taxes.
- Businesses pay tax on net income after expenses. Employees pay tax on gross income before expenses.
- The wealthy work for assets. The rich work for money. There is a critical distinction.
- Building assets, reinvesting income, and structuring wealth properly is not a loophole. It is literally how the tax code is written.
- The system is designed to reward people who build assets and penalize people who consume income.
This philosophy bridges asset protection into broader wealth education and is a major content pillar.
